How Search Engines Work

There are two different types of search engines. Crawler-based and human-based .

Crawler-based Search Engines

Search engines like google use bots to crawl, index and serve search results. Google uses a crawler called the Googlebot, which crawls URLs using algorithms to determine which sites to crawl and how often. Crawlers don't crawl every single site and can only crawl pages that are public.

After Googlebot crawls the site it tries to understand what the site is about, which is called indexing. During the indexing stage Google processes and analyzes the textual content, key content tags and attributes. Attributes such as title elements and alt attributes, images, videos, etc. During this process Google determines if the page is a duplicate of another or canonical. The canonical is what may be shown in the search results. Google clusters pages that have similar content and select the page more representative of the group which becomes the canonical.

When a user enters a query into the search Google's machine searches through the index for the most relevant pages. The relevancy of a page is determined by many factors including user's location, language, and device they are using. The user's query determines certain search features that will appear.

Human-Based Search Engines

Human-based search engines are the exact opposite of crawler-based search engines. They require a human to review and rank different pages, then enter that information into a database.

The Importance of Keywords and Descriptions as Meta Tags

The search process relies on the use of keywords. As search engines crawl and index they keep track of pages in keyword-based indexes. There are millions and millions of smaller databases each centering on a particular keyword term or phrase.

Keywords

When words are entered into the search bar the search engine matches pages to retrieve pages based on the keywords entered. Search engines measure how keywords are used in pages to determine the relevance of a particular document to a query.

In order for the keywords to rank highly for relevance you should use the keyword phrase:

  • once in title tag
  • 2-3 times with variations in body
  • at least once in alt attribute of image
  • once in the URL
  • at least once in the meta description
Do not use keywords in link anchor text pointing to other pages on your site.

Descriptions as Meta Tags

Meta descriptions are the primary source for the snippet of text that is displayed beneath a listing in the search results. They are an important part of search marketing and shows a short description of a page's content. Crafting a readable, compelling description using important keywords can draw more users. These descriptions can be any length, but should be cut to 160 characters.

4 Elements that Comprise Writing Relevant Content Links

It is confirmed by Google that links and quality content are two of the three most important rankings for SEO. Sites that you can trust will link to other trustworthy sites. Links that point to one website to another is called Inbound links. Links that connect to internal pages of the same domain are called internal links. The more internal links that are pointing to a particular page on your site will signal to Google or other search engines that this page is important.

E-E-A-T

Search engines use algorithms to evaluate pages based on links that are found. Google's Search Quality Rater Guidelines use the concept of E-E-A-T , which stands for experience , expertise , authoritativeness , and trustworthiness . If your site isn't utilizing these characteristics than your site may be seen by search engines as lower-quality. The links on your website from other sites should have qualities of E-E-A-T to earn trust and authority with search engines.

What Constitutes Good Heading Hierachy?

Semantic HTMl is use of HTML tags that define the purpose of the content they enclose to make website code more readable for developers and search engines. For example the semantic HTML tag header encloses the header section or introductory content at the top of the page. Using the right elements for the right purpose is important. Using header for introductory content only and using h1-h6 tags for main headings or sub-headings not to make the text large.

Headings and Why they are Important for SEO

H1 is used for the main topic and tells search engines what the main topic of your page is about. H2 headings divide the text into other main sections and H3 headings divide those sections further. Without these the content on your page isn't easy to scan. Clear headings show what your page is about and which sections may be relevant. When the structure of your page is clear and logical it helps search engines interpret the page content.

Using Headings Correctly

Headings should be used following a logical hierachy. You should not skip heading levels. An H2 should follow H1 and not jump directly to H3. Each heading should describe what the section is about. There should only be one H1 per page. More than one H1 heading will create confusion on what the main topic of the page is. You shouldn't use H3 headings unless they add clarity. Headers should not be used for visual styling. Each heading should be unique so that the search engines can distinguish between sections.
